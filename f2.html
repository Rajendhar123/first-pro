Before becoming the President of India, 
        he worked as an aerospace engineer at the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), a defense research 
        and development organization. He is known as Missile Man of India . Kalam especially contributed to the development 
        of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He played a key
         organizational, technical and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 . 
         In the 2002 presidential election, the Bharatiya Janata Party nominated him as a candidate and the
             opposition Congress Party supported him. 
         In that election he won against Lakshmi Sehgal who was strengthened by the Left. In his book India 2020 , 
         Kalam suggested development plans to make India a developed country by 2020. He has received many prestigious 
         awards including Bharat Ratna, 
         India's highest civilian award .
